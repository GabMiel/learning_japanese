That Phase Where I Didn’t Know What to Do Next

After learning Hiragana and getting used to Duolingo lessons, I reached this quiet point in my Japanese journey where I honestly didn’t know what to do next.

I struggled and wondered a lot on what to do next, because it felt like I was applying this knowledge for nothing. Even to this day I am struggling and juggling school work at the same time.

Time is really a precious thing but for me, it's constant overthinking of ideas of school work and what to do with my time. Thinking about the future is like hoping for a miracle. Also it would be better if it had meaning to it.

Thinking about long term goals is exhausting, but I constantly do the work every day, and that's what is important.

Even though I am watching anime and reading manhwa, I didn't try to understand the words, I am just watching and reading them for fun. Well, it's because it's fun.

Sometimes I find myself spacing out and just observing in silence. Doing nothing and just by being myself. I don't hate solitude, it just feels like I only care about myself.

I don't try having shallow conversations because I want depth. Shallow conversations are common, and it's always an open-ended question every day.

Selfish, loner, introvert, kind, respectful, giving smile to everyone when meeting someone even though I don't know their name, introspective when it comes to planning the day, yeah that's me.

But no matter what situation is happening, I'll still try to keep being myself and hoping one day I will finish the tasks that I am doing. It would be nice if someone can appreciate and read this.
