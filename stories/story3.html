My First Steps in Learning Japanese

After being inspired by anime, manhwa, and my classmates, I finally decided to take my first real steps in learning Japanese. It wasn’t just a passing thought anymore, I wanted to see how far I could go.

I started with creating my own lines in the pages of my notebook like adding vertical lines because its just ordinary notebook not a math notebook where its already have its own square spaces. It took some time but it's worth it!

Then after that, I started with Hiragana characters. I started with the word "a" or in japanese, it's "あ". I was only fill with thought of finishing or completing the page of my notebook and I didn't care much about time.

After completing that, I felt a sense of relief and a little joy of accomplishment. Well, its fun, so I decided to write as much as possible. The challenges for me was creating vertical lines on each page so it's really a hassle but i keep going.

I also have this side of being a perfectionist when creating this vertical lines because I wanted each space to be perfect and measured with a ruler, so it really took me long like 5 minutes per page, but in the end, I filled up everything on my Filler notebook.

By the end of the semester in my 2nd year of college, it took me 2 filler notebooks. I had written many Hiragana characters by the end of it. It was truly a fun journey, and I will always remember it.
