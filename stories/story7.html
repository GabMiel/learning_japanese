The Moments That Reminded Me Why I Started

There were times when I felt tired, when learning Japanese seemed too hard, and I wondered if I should stop. But then, small moments reminded me why I began this journey.

One moment was when a classmate noticed my notebook filled with Hiragana. They asked me about it, and I felt proud to share that I was learning Japanese. That simple recognition gave me strength.

Another moment was when I understood a word in anime without subtitles. It was just a small phrase, but it made me realize that my effort was paying off. I smiled, knowing I was improving.

There were also times when I wrote page after page of characters, and even though it felt repetitive, I remembered the joy of progress. Each notebook became proof that I was moving forward.
